## üöÄ How to Submit Your Contribution

### Step 1: Get Set Up
1. **Fork this repository** - Create your personal copy
2. **Clone your fork** - Get it on your local machine
3. **Create a branch** - Use a descriptive name like `add-weather-dashboard`
4. **Make your changes** - Follow the template and guidelines

### Step 2: Make Your Changes
- **For new projects:** Create a new .md file in the appropriate folder (1-Starter, 2-Builder, or 3-Expert)
- **For improvements:** Edit existing files directly
- **For examples:** Add your links to the "Example Projects" section
- **Test your changes:** Make sure formatting looks good and links work

### Step 3: Submit Your Work
1. **Commit your changes** - Write clear, descriptive commit messages
2. **Push to your fork** - Upload your changes to GitHub
3. **Create a Pull Request** - Explain what you changed and why
4. **Wait for review** - We'll check your contribution and provide feedback

## üìù What Makes a Great Contribution

### Quality Content
- **Clear objectives** - Users should know exactly what they're building
- **Realistic scope** - Projects should be completable in reasonable time

- **Logical structure** - Organize content in a way that makes sense
- **Current technologies** - Suggest modern, in-demand tools and frameworks

### User Experience
- **Beginner-friendly** - Assume readers might be new to some concepts
- **Step-by-step guidance** - Break complex features into manageable tasks
- **Practical examples** - Show real code samples and solutions
- **Encouraging tone** - Help users feel confident they can succeed

## üö´ What We Don't Accept

- **Overly complex projects** - Ideas that are unrealistic for the target level
